📝 Methods
1. Neutralising Titre Calculation — Linear Interpolation vs Curve Fitting

Two independent approaches were used to estimate neutralising antibody titres from the same raw luminescence data, and the results compared:

NT50 (Linear Interpolation) For each replicate column, a neutralising titre was estimated by 50%/90% reduction of the NSC per replicate. Linear interpolation was performed between the two closest dilution points bounding the estimated dilution point. Titres were calculated per replicate and then the mean was calculated across the triplicate to give the final NT50/NT90%.
IC50 Titre (Curve Fitting) A four-parameter logistic sigmoid was fitted to the triplicate-mean neutralisation percentages across all dilutions (see Curve Fitting Methods). The IC50 (log₂ dilution at 50% neutralisation) was converted to a neutralisating titre via 2−IC50.
2. Sample Matching and Correlation

NT50 and IC50 titre values were matched by pseudotype virus name and sample ID. Where no exact match was found, samples were matched in sequential order (the order for curve fitting and linear interpolation is the same). Both titre values were log₁₀-transformed prior to analysis.

Pearson's correlation coefficient (r) was calculated and a linear regression model was fitted to obtain R², representing the proportion of variance. The strength of association was classified using set thresholds: weak (|r| < 0.4), moderate (0.4 ≤ |r| < 0.7), or strong (|r| ≥ 0.7). The coefficient of determination was classified as negligible (R² < 0.25), weak (0.25 ≤ R² < 0.50), moderate (0.50 ≤ R² < 0.75), or substantial (R² ≥ 0.75).

3. Disagreement Classification

The log₂ fold difference between the two methods was calculated for each sample as:

Log₂ Fold Difference = log₂(NT50 / IC50 Titre)

A log₂ fold difference of 0 indicates perfect agreement. Positive values indicate that linear interpolation gave a higher titre, while negative values indicate that curve fitting produced a higher titre. Samples with an absolute log₂ fold difference greater than 1, by the default setting, (i.e. a greater than 2-fold difference) were flagged for review.

Common causes of disagreement include incomplete or atypical curves, flat titrations where the sigmoid fit is poorly constrained, or edge cases where the neutralisation threshold falls outside the measured dilution range.

The five samples with the highest absolute log₂ fold difference are reported in the Top Mismatches table. All data is available for download from the "Download Data Files" section above for further review and analysis.
