📝 Methods
1. Titre Derivation — Two Independent Methods

Two independent approaches were used to estimate neutralising antibody titres from the same raw luminescence data, and the results compared to assess inter-method agreement:

NT50 (Linear Interpolation) For each replicate column, the luminescence value corresponding to 50% of the no-serum control (NSC) was identified. Linear interpolation between the two flanking dilution points was used to estimate the dilution at which the 50% threshold was crossed. The three per-replicate NT50 values were then averaged to produce a single NT50 per quadrant.
IC50 Titre (Curve Fitting) A four-parameter logistic sigmoid was fitted to the triplicate-mean neutralisation percentages across all dilutions (see Curve Fitting Methods). The IC50 — the log₂ dilution at 50% neutralisation — was converted to a reciprocal dilution titre via 2−IC50.
2. Sample Matching and Correlation

NT50 and IC50 titre values were matched by pseudotype (virus) name and sample identifier. Where no exact match was found, case-insensitive matching with trimmed whitespace was attempted. Both titre values were log₁₀-transformed prior to correlation analysis to account for the log-normal distribution of neutralisation titres.

Pearson's correlation coefficient (r) was computed on the log₁₀-transformed values. A linear regression model (log₁₀ IC50 ~ log₁₀ NT50) was fitted to obtain R², representing the proportion of variance in IC50 titres explained by NT50 titres. The strength of association was classified using standard thresholds: weak (|r| < 0.4), moderate (0.4 ≤ |r| < 0.7), or strong (|r| ≥ 0.7). The coefficient of determination was classified as negligible (R² < 0.25), weak (0.25 ≤ R² < 0.50), moderate (0.50 ≤ R² < 0.75), or substantial (R² ≥ 0.75).

3. Disagreement Classification

The log₂ fold difference between the two methods was calculated for each sample as:

Log₂ Fold Difference = log₂(NT50 / IC50 Titre)

A log₂ fold difference of 0 indicates perfect agreement. Positive values indicate that the linear interpolation method yielded a higher titre, while negative values indicate that curve fitting produced a higher titre. Samples with an absolute log₂ fold difference greater than 1 (i.e. a greater than 2-fold difference) were flagged for review. This threshold is standard in serological titre comparisons and corresponds to a single dilution step discrepancy.

Common causes of disagreement include incomplete or atypical dose–response curves, flat titrations where the sigmoid fit is poorly constrained, or edge cases where the 50% neutralisation threshold falls outside the measured dilution range.

The five samples with the highest absolute log₂ fold difference are reported in the Top Mismatches table above. The curve fitting quality flag ("Good" or "Unstable") is included alongside each mismatch to aid interpretation — an "Unstable" flag indicates that the sigmoid fit for that sample had one or more parameters pressed against its bound constraint, and the IC50 titre should be treated with caution.
