📝 Method

Neutralising Antibody Titre Determination by Linear Interpolation

Neutralising antibody titres were determined at 50% and 90% neutralisation levels (NT50 and NT90).

Threshold Calculation

For each replicate column, the titre was estimated relative to the NSC luminescence::

TargetNT50 = luminescence(cps)NSC × 0.50
TargetNT90 = luminescence(cps)NSC × 0.10

Linear Interpolation

The neutralising titre for each replicate was estimated by linear interpolation between the two assay dilution points on either side of to the titre estimation:

NT = xi + (Target − yi) / (yi+1 − yi) × (xi+1 − xi)

where xi and xi+1 are the serum dilution factors at the two adjacent dilution points, and yi and yi+1 are the corresponding luminescence values.

Titre Calculation

Neutralising titres were calculated for each of replicate within the assay and the mean calculated for each triplicate at 50% and 90% neutralisation thresholds. If the titre was outwith the bounds of the dilution series in the excel template file (cells A5-A12) then the titre was reported as either <A5 or >A12. Boundary values were excluded from the box plot visualisation and from downstream analyses.

Box Plot

Neutralising titres are compared for each pseudotype in a Tukey box plot (log10scale). Boxes span the interquartile range (Q1–Q3), the horizontal line is the median and whiskers reach the furthest data point within 1.5× IQR of the box. Points beyond the whiskers are plotted individually as outliers. All individual averaged titres are overlaid for transparency.
