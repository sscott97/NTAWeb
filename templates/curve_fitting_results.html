📝 Methods
1. Data Extraction and Normalisation

Data was extracted from the processed excel file and the mean was calculated for each triplicate.

Percentage neutralisation at each dilution step was then calculated as:

% Neutralisation = 100 × (1 − x̄dilution / x̄NSC)

where x̄dilution is the mean triplicate value at a given dilution and x̄NSC is the mean luminescence of the corresponding no-serum control wells. Dilution factors were converted to a log₂ scale (−log₂ of the dilution fraction) for model fitting.

2. Four-Parameter Sigmoid Model

A four-parameter logistic (4PL) sigmoid function was fitted to the neutralisation data for each sample–virus combination:

f(x) = Lower + (Upper − Lower) / (1 + exp(−Slope × (x − IC50)))

The four estimated parameters are:

Lower Minimum asymptote (% neutralisation at low antibody concentration), bounded [−50, 80]
Upper Maximum asymptote (% neutralisation at high antibody concentration), bounded [0, 100]
Slope Slope of the transition, bounded [0.001, 10]
IC50 Inflection point — the log₂ dilution at the sigmoid midpoint, bounded [−20, 0]
3. Parameter Estimation

Parameters were estimated by non-linear least squares regression using the Levenberg–Marquardt algorithm (nlsLM function, minpack.lm R package), with a maximum of 200 iterations. Starting values were set to Lower = 0, Upper = 100, Slope = 1, and IC50 = −8. Parameter bounds were enforced to constrain solutions within biologically plausible ranges (see table above).

4. Titre Calculation

The neutralising antibody titre was derived from the fitted IC50 by converting from log₂ dilution back to a reciprocal dilution factor:

Titre = 2−IC50
5. Goodness of Fit and Quality Assessment

Goodness of fit was assessed using a pseudo-R² statistic:

R² = max(0, 1 − RSS / TSS)

where RSS is the residual sum of squares between observed and predicted neutralisation values, and TSS is the total sum of squares about the mean.

Samples that resulted in an R² < 0.5 were reported as Outside Limit of Detection (LOD) and given NA vaues and exluded from generated graphs.

A quality flag of "Unstable" was assigned when any fitted parameter reached its upper or lower bound constraint, indicating that the optimiser could not find a well-defined solution within the bounded parameter space. All other successful fits were flagged as "Good". Unstable fits should be interpreted with caution, as the resulting IC50 and titre values may not reliably represent the true neutralising activity of the sample.
